The problem

The data is the actual hazard, not the queue

Veggie chilli (vegan?)
"celery. may contain traces of nuts"
Thai green curry
"see chef"
Katsu sando (special)
"ask counter"
Fruit pot
"null"

This is the brief's own starter menu, not invented. A server who started Monday can't parse this in thirty seconds. Neither should an AI have to guess.

The design

Two taps. One of three honest answers.

Safe

Confirmed clean against the allergen asked about.

Unsafe

Confirmed to contain it.

Ask staff

Data is ambiguous or missing. Never guessed as safe.

Not a confidence score. A score just moves the guessing problem onto someone with thirty seconds, not the time to interpret "72% safe." "Ask staff" is the correct answer whenever the data itself doesn't support a claim of safe, and it can never be silently overridden.

Technology choices

Two routes. One touches an LLM.

Button taps skip the LLM entirely, a dict lookup under 200ms. GPT-4o-mini only runs for free-text questions.

The questions I expect to defend

Why these decisions, specifically

Why not a confidence score?
It moves the guessing problem onto someone with thirty seconds. Three plain states don't.
Why not agentic, why no LangGraph or multi-agent setup?
This is one bounded classification decision, not a multi-step workflow. Agentic complexity here is tool-stacking, not creativity.
Why no vector database?
30 structured records, not a large unstructured corpus. Fuzzy string matching solves it for free, with no risk of confidently returning the wrong dish.
